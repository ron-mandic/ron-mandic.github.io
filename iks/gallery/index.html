Doaa Al Zamel

Doaa's early life

Doaa was born in Daraa, Syria, which is located about two hours away from Damascus. She grew up with her five sisters and one brother. Her father worked as a barber. When she was six years old, protests against President Bashar al-Assad began, and when she was 16, the Syrian civil war broke out in 2011. As a result, her family faced significant challenges.

Fleeing

After their father's barbershop was destroyed, the family made the decision to flee to Egypt in November 2012, seeking safety and stability. This move was prompted by the escalating violence and instability in their home country. Their journey to Egypt marked the beginning of their difficult and uncertain life as refugees.

Masa and Malak

Doaa holds the two surviving children, Mara (nine-month old) and Malak (18-month old), in a floating ring, keeping them above water.

Doaa Al Zamel

Doaa was one of the 11 survivors out of 500 people in a shipwreck in the Mediterranean Sea in 2014. She was on an overcrowded boat along with many other refugees when the boat capsized, resulting in the drowning of numerous individuals. However, she managed to survive and rescued two children from drowning during the incident by keeping them afloat until they were rescued by a rescue ship.

My Hope

The story of Doaa Al Zamel and her heroic rescue was documented in the book "Doaa: My Hope for a Better Future," written by Melissa Fleming. The book narrates her escape from Syria, her harrowing experience on the boat, and her journey to Europe. It sheds light on the challenging circumstances and dangers faced by many refugees attempting to find safety by crossing the Mediterranean Sea.

The Ship

The "M/S Prinsesse Ragnhild" was a Norwegian ship in 2012 that played a vital role in rescuing refugees in distress at sea.

Doaa and Bassem

Al Zamel got engaged to a man named Bassem. After the change in Egyptian leadership from Mohamed Morsi to Abdel Fattah el-Sisi, conditions worsened for refugees like Bassem and Al Zamel. In pursuit of a better quality of life, they made the decision to flee to Sweden. To begin their journey, Bassem used his life savings of US$2,500 to pay people smugglers who promised to transport them both by boat across the Mediterranean Sea to Italy. Unfortunately, Bassem lost his life during the shipwreck that caused the boat to capsize.

Rescue

They were discovered by the crew of the "M/S Prinsesse Ragnhild," a Norwegian ship that was involved in refugee rescue operations. The crew of the rescue ship responded swiftly and saved Doaa Al Zamel and the two children from the water. They were brought on board the ship, provided with medical care, but sadly Malak died five hours after the rescue.
